Basotho traditional music & dance

Traditional music and dance are important in Lesotho.People use music and dance to celebrate,tell stories,and show respect during ceremonies. Below there are discription of traditional Basotho dances and instruments.


Dances:


Mokhibo

Mokhibo is a traditional Basotho dance performed by women.women kneel on the ground and move their shoulders rhythmically, and sing traditional song.

Litolobonya

A traditional Basotho dance performed by women during celebrations and special gatherings.

Mohobelo

Mohobelo is a traditional Basotho dance performed by men.In this dance, men stamp their feet,jump,and move with strength to the beat of music.

Liphotha

Liphotha is a traditional basotho dance performed by young men.Men that perform liphotha should wear gumboots.It is a dance that includes jumping,stamping,and quick movements with the beat of gumboots.


Tenye

Tenye is a traditional basotho dance performed by women during ceremonies.

Ts'ipu

Ts'ipu is a traditional dance performed by young girls and women during ceremonies.I involves light foot movements and hip shaking,dancers often sing traditional songs while dancing.

Motebuko

Motebuko is a traditional Basotho welcoming song and dance performrd to greet or honor important guest.it is performed by singing ,clapping and dancing.

Senyamo

Senyamo is a traditional Basotho dance performed by men during initiation ceremonies or cultural events.the dance includes strong foot stamping,clapping and singing.

Moteliso

Moteliso is a traditional Basotho dance performed by women,it is performed during joyful celebrations.The dance involves singing ,ululating and gentle movements.


Instruments:


Mamokhorong

Mamokhorong is a Basotho traditional music instrument made from stick,single wire string and metal container,the sound is produced by friction and the player controls the pitch by stopping the string with fingers.

Lekope

Lekope is a basotho traditional music instrument made from a hollowed reed with a string horsehair,plucked with a finger,and sounded against the palyer's mouth to modulate the tone

Lesiba

Lesiba is a traditional music instrument made from a long stick ,a taut string,and a flattened quill attached to the end of string,which is set in motion by the player's breath to create melodic sound.

Sekupu

Sekupu is a music instrument,traditionally large ,hollowed wooden drum,which evolved into a double-headed drum made from a metal container with cow or goat skin heads.It is played with short,padded sticks and serves as a grounding force in sotho music and dance traditions.

sekebeku

Sekebeku is a wooden or metal jaw harp used in Lesotho for personal asmusement and travel.Wood or metal,provide the vibrating element and are chosen for the specific sound and durability desired for the instrument,which is held against the mouth to create music through resonance.

Thomo

Thomo is a traditional Basotho musical instrument,it is a bow with a single string made of horsehair,a calabash attched as a resonator near the end of the bow and thin reed used to strike the string.The instrument is typically held upright by the player in their left hand,with the calabash resonator pressed against their chest to produce sound.

Setolo-tolo

Setolo-tolo is a Basotho musical instrument made from bambooor wood for the braced bow-like structure and often bound with plant fiber,copper wire or gut to create the string.

khabulu-bulu

Khabulu-bulu instrument is made from metal container and is played by hitting or shaking it to make a sound.

Lekhitla la lenaka

Lekhitla la lenaka is a traditional musical instrument made from a cow horns.It is used like a trumpet.The player blows into the small end,it makes a deep,loud sound.

Lehlaka

Lehlaka is a traditional instrument made from a reed or thin stick,it is played by blowing air through one end to make a soft;whistling sound.It is played by young boys.

Moropa

Moropa is a traditional instrument made from a hollow wooden body covered with animal skin on top.It is played by beating covered hollow wooden with hands or sticks.

Sekebeku sa ts'epe

Sekebeku sa ts'epe is a traditional instrument made from one or more metal strings stretched over a piece of wood or a tin can.It is played by plucking the strings with fingers to make sound.

Lekhitlane la lesapo

Traditional Basotho musical instrument made froma bone.It is played by blowing air through it,like a fluete or whistle,to make sound.

Lekolulo

Lekolulo is a traditional instrument made from a reed or thin stick and played by blowing air through one end while covering and uncovering a small holes to make different sounds.
